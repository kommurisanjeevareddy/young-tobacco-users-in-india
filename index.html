YOUNG TOBACCO USERS IN INDIA

TABLEAU VISUALIZATION

ABOUT DATASET

The dataset of young tobacco users in India from data.gov.in is a collection of data on the prevalence of tobacco use among school-going children aged 13-15 years in India. The data was collected in 2019 by the International Institute for Population Sciences (IIPS) under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW). The survey was conducted in all states and union territories of India, and included a sample of over 97,000 students.
The data can be used to visualize the prevalence of tobacco use among young people in India, and to identify the factors that contribute to tobacco use among young people. The data can also be used to develop interventions to prevent tobacco use among young people.

STORY TELLING

In India, a team of data analysts and visualization experts used compelling visualizations to highlight the concerning rise of tobacco use among the youth. Their engaging charts, maps, and infographics showcased the prevalence, regional variations, and underlying factors driving youth tobacco consumption. Their impactful visuals sparked awareness, drove policy changes, and inspired collaborative efforts to combat this pressing issue, leading to a decline in young tobacco users and a healthier future for India's youth.
